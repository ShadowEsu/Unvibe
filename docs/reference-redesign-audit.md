# Marketing redesign audit — DataAnnotation finance reference

Date: 2026-07-16  
Reference: `https://www.dataannotation.tech/finance`

## What the reference does well

- Opens with one oversized, centered promise and a single obvious action.
- Alternates decisive background fields instead of placing every idea in another pale card.
- Gives each scroll chapter one job, creating a readable editorial rhythm.
- Uses large numbered sections and sticky demonstrations to turn explanation into a visual sequence.
- Keeps interface mockups simple enough to understand in a few seconds.
- Repeats calls to action only at natural decision points.
- Uses motion to reveal relationships, not as ambient decoration.

At 1440 px the reference measures roughly 13,900 px over ten major chapters. Its hero headline is about 94 px and later section headings about 64 px. The strongest pages are the ones with a clear field change and a single dominant composition.

## What should not be copied

- Its wording, illustrations, exact layouts, brand colors, and compensation-focused story.
- Its mobile behavior: at 390 px, the inspected page retains a wide desktop canvas and is visibly cropped.
- Its high animation density as a goal by itself. Uncode needs fewer, more instructive transitions.
- Any implication that listed editors, agents, or platforms are official partners.

## Problems in the current Unvibe page

- Fifteen sections stretch to roughly 19,500 px at 1440 px, so the strongest ideas arrive too slowly.
- Repeated pale cards and oversized empty gaps flatten the hierarchy.
- Product demonstrations are visually small relative to the surrounding whitespace.
- The opening copy explains the category before making the emotional promise.
- The current beta form asks qualification questions before the visitor has joined.
- Several scenes imply capabilities more confidently than current product readiness supports.
- Mobile is functional, but the announcement, hero, CTA, and code window feel crowded.

## Strengths worth preserving

- The core line, “AI writes the code. Unvibe helps you understand it,” is immediate and differentiated.
- Existing code, explanation, project-map, and privacy scenes make the product tangible.
- The lavender and warm neutral palette feels approachable without relying on an “AI glow.”
- Legal, privacy, data-control, and account-deletion routes already provide a solid trust foundation.
- The site already includes reduced-motion handling, keyboard-friendly controls, dark mode, and responsive foundations.
- Current copy is generally careful about beta readiness, pricing, AI accuracy, and provider limitations.

## Chosen direction: pixelated learning

Uncode will use its existing cream, lavender, purple, and charcoal palette with crisp one-pixel borders, blocky progress markers, and editorial typography. The pixel language represents understanding being built one concept at a time; it is not retro game decoration.

The emotional frame is: **Do not feel guilty about vibe coding. Make the code yours.** The product statement remains: **AI writes the code. Uncode helps you understand it.**

Motion will be reserved for the product loop, level switching, and the context ladder. Every animated scene has manual controls, pauses outside the viewport, and becomes static under reduced motion.

## Final launch-page sequence

1. Hero promise and immediate controlled product demonstration
2. The comprehension problem: could you explain the feature tomorrow?
3. Numbered loop: select, understand, retain
4. Five explanation levels with an interactive example
5. Signature context ladder: line to concept
6. Works beside the tools developers already use, without partnership claims
7. Each project becomes a curriculum with cautious evidence language
8. Clear audience fit for learners, builders, reviewers, and leads
9. Privacy architecture and controls, limited to implemented or explicitly in-development behavior
10. Three-field private-beta signup with optional questions only after success
11. Concise FAQ and final call to action

The result should fit in about 11,500–14,000 px at desktop widths, preserve generous breathing room, and remain fully composed at 390 px without horizontal cropping.
